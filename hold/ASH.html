Project
14 - 10 - 18

ASH

Problem: Map out the touch points that will be required to assist people when interacting with autonomous vehicles in future cities.

We began our journey by researching the current politacl climate and policies that outline the core issues pertinent to Sydney-siders in 2018. through our research the team discovered a re-occuring catalyst for a myriad of problems - Domestic Violence (DV) and so we decided to focus or energy here.

Solution: After focusing on how autonomy could best help those most affected by DV we came up with a concept that combined a mobile app with an in-car interface that would provide the user with the skills and resources they needed to gain access to help and most importantly protect them and their anonymity.


After deciding on a problem space we began our first hand research by triangualing the major stakeholders within this topic; Police, Housing Officials and Social Workers. The aim of our research was to determine the major issues within the systems set-up to improve the lives of DV sufferers, in both the government and private sectors. In doing this we hoped to learn about the major pain points within this process and assess where we could be most effective in designing a component of the solution.

We utilised; Open-Ended Interviews, Contextual Mapping and Analysis when ingaging with particpants in their place of work. I interviewed police officers and detectives that are heavily involved in the DV unit and the Sergeant in charge of the unit.

Once this data was collected and synthesised the team began amalgamating all the knowledge we had gained and began to form a deeper understanding of the space.

We struggled to initiate concepts from the information that we had gained and so we went back to the basics and looked up excercises from Design Think Make Break Repeat (Tomitsch et al, 2017). We began by generating a list of all the vehicles we could think of and then trialing fprced assosciations to generate over 60 ideas, which we then solidified by turning them into XYZ statements.
Compiling our list of preliminary designs and after a rigorous process of elimination we landed on three designs that best suited our archtypal person and we could move forward.

The next step was to futher solidify these concepts so that the entire team was on the same page and we used brainwriting 6-3-5 to help accomplish this. Then we each developed a storyboard to help visualise the issue we were trying to solve and how the concepts fit into the space.


With our concepts in their embryotic stages the next stap of our design journey was to map out the cor components and screens that would be needed for each idea. When then moved onto generating a variety of wireframes ranging from low to mid fidelity.

We tested paper prototypes with participants representative of our personas, recording their comments and actions to analysis and help improve our designs. From our initial testes we extrapolated from the results that the most useable and useful design was the "Autonomous Secure Housing" design and so we decided to develope it further.

Taking our initial hand-drawn wireframes and developing all the necassary screens in both the mobile and in-car interface, we then tested, iterated, tested and iterated again until we were happy with the layout, phrasing and components we had developed.


Before we could begin prototyping, a style guide needed to be created so that we could turn our basic B/W wireframes from boxes and lines into the final product. We designed the colour scheme, font selection, spacing and icon selection that would enable us to create a cohesive design for both the mobile and in-car dispplays.

Next, we began prototyping or designs using FRAMER, a software that combines visual design with coding (Coffee Script) to develope micro-interactions and transitions for any design. The process was time consuming compared to using Invision or similar products, but it allowed us to personalise our design and provided us with a lot more freedom.

Once we had built the majority of this prototype we tested again with users to make sure that we hadn't missed any key features and with a few minor changes the final prototype was ready to present.

The final concept for the Autonomous Secure Housing (ASH) system is a mobile app that allows users to order an autonomous bus to a set location at anytime, removing them from a dangerous situation at home. The app contains a "HIDDEN MODE" that instantly changes the screen to a simple game until a code is entered to protect their privacy.

The in-car component of the project was focused on making the user feel as comfortable as possible in this new environment. We achieved this by providing constant feedback about where the bus was in relation to geography and it's immedeate surroundings. The interface utilises window pop-ups that lay over the main screen to provide common controls and theses screens can be expanded to provide more control if desired. The design also contains information on processes that can be used to protect the user in future scemnarios and also contact details of services they can contact to ask for more help.


Everyone’s experience of domestic violence is different. Whatever your situation, it is important to know that help and support is available.
