Design Process

The TagMCA project was a group assessment I worked on in early 2018 as part of the User Experience Design Studio. The aim of this project was to design a digital/tangible/spatial experience that builds upon the collection of the Museum of Contemporary Art, Sydney. The solution needed to improve the visitor experience, fulfill educational goals of the institution and help the MCA to engage with new audiences. The experience also had to be interwoven with the context; it could not exist without the collection and environment of its context.

We began the process by examining the space in detail and the people that frequented the MCA. We questioned museum curators about their experiences, photographed and mapped our own journey's through the space and finally shadowed a variety of patrons to gain a better insight into the flow and salient aspects of the space.

From this examination of the space the group decided that the most appropriate group of people to target our attention to was 'Sydney-siders' aged 20-35 who had been to the MCA once before but had never returned. As a result of our previous primary research our group decided to redefine the Design Brief and devise a research method that would allow us to gather in-depth qualitative information from the archetype of our focus audience.

Research Plan

Museums and art galleries are intended as educational leisure settings; they seek to weave educational and aesthetic elements in a social context (Forrest, 2014). If this is the intent - why do some visitors feel these settings do not enable them to have a leisurely time with a friend? Audiences seek informal, playful settings for an outing. They love the shared reflection aspect of an art gallery, finding obscure details and discussing pieces with another. The gallery presents barriers to this: content isn't always enough to keep them going, and a lack of knowledge of the art world leads them to believe they can't enjoy it to the fullest. We seek to utilise what the MCA already possesses: a breadth of curated modern art content, expansive and self-exploratory indoor exhibition rooms, the striking exterior architecture and the harbour-side locale it resides in. We will couple this with our users needs and layer a technological aspect to create an experience that removes these preconceived barriers and turns the MCA into a place audiences love to just hang out.
  
We aim to shift the perception of the MCA as a solely aesthetic and passive experience to one that visitors view as a leisurely, socially connected environment. We seek to augment this aesthetic experience with interactivity and play so that the museum retains its inspirational and educational qualities in a way that visitors will want to return to with another. We hope to break down preconceived barriers that guests create; the museum can be a stifling environment that does not enable social leisure and that you need to “know” art to have a good time. We will achieve this by utilising what the MCA already possesses: a breadth of curated modern art content, expansive and self-exploratory indoor exhibition rooms, the striking exterior architecture and the harbour-side locale it resides in. We will interweave this with our insights into what visitors require from a place to warrant them to return to conceptualise a digital, tangible experience that emboldens their sociality and makes them want to visit time and time again. Come again, bring a friend.
